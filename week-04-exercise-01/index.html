My Dev Tools Vandalism

I used browser developer tools to temporarily "vandalize" a website. Below is a screenshot showing my changes. These edits only appeared on my computer and did not affect the actual website.

What I "Vandalized"

I chose to vandalize the homepage of microsoft.com using my browser’s developer tools. It was surprisingly fun and gave me a deeper understanding of how websites are built and styled.

  • I found a promotional banner that originally said something like “Save up to $600.00” and changed it to something more playful: “Surface Laptop, Unicorns, and more” It instantly gave the page a whimsical twist and made me laugh.
  • I used the Styles panel to change: background-color to orange border to 8px dashed teal transform to rotate(+4deg) These changes made the banner look like it was tilted and taped onto the page with crayon scribbles. I also experimented with font size and color to exaggerate the headline.
  • The contrast between Microsoft’s sleek, professional design and my goofy edits made it hilarious. It felt like sneaking a doodle into a corporate brochure.
